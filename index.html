The Biggest K-POP groups

Blackpink

Blackpink debuted in 2016 as a 4 member girl group. They are under YG Entertainment which is one of the biggest K-POP entertainment agencies and this helped them shoot into stardom and success instantly. Within the course of 7 years, they've broken countless records. They are the first female K-POP female artists to make it on the cover of Billboard. They are the first K-POP female artists to perform at coachelle. From 2019-2022 they broke a record of biggest music video debut with each of their comebacks. Blackpink is a phenomenon and they are going to have many more record breaking years with success.

BTS

BTS debuted in 2013 under BigHit Entertainment. BigHit started out as a small company and BTS unexpectedly shot into stardom making BigHit Entertainment now known as HYBE Entertainment, one of the biggest K-POP entertainments today. BTS started reaching their success in 2015 with their new album "The Most Beautiful Moment in Life." In 2017, they went on to be the first K-POP artists to ever perform at the AMA's and win a BillBoard. In 2018, their album "Love Yourself" managed to debut at #1 on the BillBoard Top 200 making them the first to chart #1 as a K-POP group. By 2020, they were the biggest group in the world, even getting compared to The Beatles with how popular they have gotten. Their single "Dynamite" spent the longest weeks on the Hot 100 Billboard charts and also managed to surpass "Gangnam Style" by Psy. Dynamite also broke a record of most views in 24 hours racking up 101 million views within the time frame. In 2021, they released "Butter" and that music video broke another 24 hour view record, racking up 108 million views in 24 hours! By this time of year, they were regularly performing all over the world including America. Their shows were constatly sold out. Their world tour that had gotten cancelled due to COVID was projected to be one of the biggest selling world tours in history.

TWICE

TWICE debuted in 2015 under JYP Entertainment as a 9 member girl group. JYP has been one of the biggest K-POP entertainment agencies for a while, so TWICE reaching to stardom did not take long. Their debut song "Like Ooh-Ahh" had gotten people talking about them. By their second song "Cheer Up", they managed to receive the title as the "Nation's Girl Group" in South Korea. They currently have the most Youtube videos that have surpassed 100 million views. Their subscriber count is at 16.4 million. To this day they are continuing to serve the public with great hits and comebacks.
